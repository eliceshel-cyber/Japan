📍 Maps
🌋
Option B — Yakedake Volcano Hike (optional, harder group or solo)
Active volcano, the only peak in Kamikochi feasibly done as a full day hike. 5–6 hrs total, 870m elevation gain. Panoramic views over the entire valley at the summit. Active steam vents and sulfur smell near the top.
Kamikochi Route — starts at Kappa Bridge. Steel ladders and chains on upper sections. Mountain hut (Yakedake-goya) midway — helmets to borrow free.
Nakanoyu Route — starts near Taisho-ike bus stop. No ladders, straightforward forest climb. Better if anyone not comfortable with heights. No water or facilities — bring everything.
⚠ Check Japan Meteorological Agency (jma.go.jp) for volcanic activity alert the morning of the hike. Warnings also posted at the trailhead.
